  • Eternal
    Sri Hamsa Bhagwan
    The divine origin of the Nimbarka Sampradaya begins with Sri Krishna as Hamsa avatara. This eternal source represents the transcendent aspect of the Supreme Lord, initiating the spiritual lineage that flows through time to guide devotees on the path of divine love.
  • Primordial
    Sanakadi Kumaras
    The four eternal sons of Brahma - Sanaka, Sanandana, Sanatana, and Sanat-kumara - received divine knowledge directly from the Supreme. Due to their connection with these sages, the lineage is also known as Sanakadi Sampradaya or Kumara Sampradaya.
  • Ancient
    Sri Narada Muni
    The divine sage Narada, celestial messenger and devotee of Vishnu, carried forward the sacred teachings. Known for his devotional songs and spreading bhakti throughout the universe, he transmitted the philosophy of divine love to earthly teachers.
  • 7th-13th Century CE
    Sri Nimbarkacharya
    The founding acharya who established the Dvaitadvaita philosophy of simultaneous difference and non-difference. Born as an incarnation of Sudarshana Chakra, he systematized the worship of Radha-Krishna and composed foundational texts including commentaries on Brahma Sutras and Bhagavad Gita.
  • 1443-1543 CE
    Sri Harivyasa Devacharya
    The great saint-poet who revolutionized the sampradaya through his devotional songs and social reforms. He spread the path of Radha-Krishna bhakti among all classes of society and established the Devacharya tradition that continues unbroken to this day in Vrindavan and beyond.
